## Lede

I am Praise Prince — an engineer by training, a reader by appetite, and a photographer by approximate accident.

## Bio

I keep this site the way some people keep a garden: it is not the point, exactly, but it is where the work of paying attention happens. Most of what I publish here is small. A photograph from a walk, a fragment overheard, a note on something in computer science or physics that struck me as more beautiful than it had any right to be. Once or twice a season, an essay — longer, slower, less sure of itself.

I write between Lagos and Cambridge. I shoot mostly on a Yashica T4 my father gave me. I read more philosophy than is good for an engineer, and more code than is good for an essayist. The two halves do not always speak, but they share a room.

This place is private without being secret. It has no analytics, no email capture, no newsletter form. You are simply here. *Thank you for that.*

## Code

This is a small static site built with Astro. Most pages are Markdown files with frontmatter, because I wanted writing, photo rolls, walks, and notes to be easy to edit without turning the site into an app.

The design leans on a few reusable tags and layouts rather than a CMS: essays, photos, walks, fragments, and desk entries each get just enough structure to stay organized while still feeling like a notebook.

The code lives on [GitHub](https://github.com/praiseprince/blog).
