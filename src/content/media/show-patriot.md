I had this recommended to me three times before I gave in. I owe each of those people a tea. *Patriot* is the kind of show that has been engineered to be misunderstood by trailers — every scene is doing four things at once, and the scene that finally explains the thing usually arrives forty minutes after you needed it.

It is also extremely funny, in a deadpan, slightly Coen-brothers way. The songs the main character writes about his classified missions, performed in coffee houses for confused audiences, are the kind of joke that you tell yourself you should not laugh at, and then laugh at anyway. The episode where the entire engineering staff of a pipe factory tries to keep a bag of money from being noticed is, I think, the funniest single sequence of television I have ever seen.

Underneath the comedy is a tender argument about labour, and about what a person does when their inner life has been irreparably broken. The show does not flinch from that, and it does not solve it. It just keeps the character moving forward, badly, and lets the camera be patient with him.

It is on Amazon and the algorithm seems to hide it. Two seasons. Watch it.
