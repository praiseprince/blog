Some films are about something. *Stalker* is *the experience of something* — the way the dripping in the Zone is the experience of waiting, the way the famous tracking shot over the water is the experience of having to look. I do not think there is another director who has done this with the same patience.

What I had not expected was how much of it is *talk*. Three men in a wet room, arguing about whether the thing they are about to attempt is worth attempting. I think the entire film could be reduced to that argument and lose almost nothing. The Zone is the argument made spatial.

I will watch it again, but not soon. It is one of those films that asks for distance between viewings; an annual visit at most.
