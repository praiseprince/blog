This is a book I have started twice and am finally finishing on the second pass. I am writing this review the way I am reading the book — in increments, with gaps, returning when I can.

The first thing to know is that the title is misleading on purpose. *Why Fish Don't Exist* sounds like pop-science contrarianism, and the first thirty pages let you believe that is what you are getting — a clever taxonomy puzzle. It is not. It is a book about a 19th-century scientist (David Starr Jordan) who lost his life's work in the 1906 San Francisco earthquake and rebuilt it, and what that rebuilding cost — both him and other people.

I will keep adding to this as I get further. There is more to say about the middle chapters than I have figured out yet. Come back.
