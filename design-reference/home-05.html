On the habit of looking twice.

There is a small, almost ceremonial discipline I am trying to keep this year: when something catches my eye, I look at it twice. Not to confirm — to refuse the first answer. On photography as a slowing-down, on the engineer's version of the same discipline, and on Bachelard.

Tuesday · Nov 04 · 12:20Note · Engineering6 min

A small note on Verlet integration.

Most game engines do not simulate velocities. They simulate positions, twice. The trick is older than you would expect — eighteenth-century, give or take — and it is, in its way, a small philosophical statement. 

// Position Verlet.
const next = {
  x: 2 * pos.x - prev.x + acc.x * dt * dt,
  y: 2 * pos.y - prev.y + acc.y * dt * dt,
};
prev = pos;
pos  = next;
