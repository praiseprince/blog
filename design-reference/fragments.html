Things I wrote down
that were not yet anything.

Notebook scraps from this month. Numbered, mostly unedited. I am suspicious of any that have already turned into ideas.

№ 088.01 · Nov 02

The kind of friend who asks one more question than is polite — that is the only kind worth keeping.

№ 088.02 · Nov 03 · overheard, danfo

"My problem," the woman behind me said, "is not that the world is unfair. It is that nobody has explained to me on whose behalf it is unfair."

She got off at Obalende and did not look back.

№ 088.04 · note to self

— Stop using the word interesting. It is doing the work three more honest words could do.

№ 088.05 · half-poem
      the harmattan came
      in the evening, in the harbor —
      somebody's washing
      was forgotten on the line
      and the wind made a flag of it
      for nobody in particular.
№ 088.06 · Nov 10 · midnight

The problem with productivity advice is that it is almost always written by people who have not yet lost anything large. I would like to read more from people who have. They tend to be quieter, which is itself the point.

№ 088.07 · screen-grab, anonymised

> the assumption that anyone could
> actually become the kind of person who
> eats well, sleeps well, & reads widely
> without first being unbearable about it
> is one of the great unfunded fictions
> of late-modern life.

№ 088.09 · reading, Bachelard

"The mind needs to be against the mind."

— underlined twice, then a third time the next day.

№ 088.10 · Nov 14

Three good hours of work today. The rest of the day I made a small ceremony of doing nothing. I think the ceremony is the part the work needs.

№ 088.11 · Nov 17 · long fragment

On the bus home: a small argument about whether suffering is necessary for art. I find the question almost embarrassing now. Of course it is not necessary. But it is not not necessary, either — and the people most insistent that it isn't are usually people who have only ever made very tidy work.

Better question: what kinds of suffering does which work require? The answer is mostly boredom.
