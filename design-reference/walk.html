Walks · entry 21

A walk along
Third Mainland,
before the bridge woke up.

06:14 → 07:42
5.2 km · clear
Lagos Lagoon side
Notebook 11, p. 48–53
Observations are dated; quotations are exact; smells are reconstructed.
06:14
The first stretch.

The bridge is never really still, but at this hour it pretends. The lagoon underneath is the colour of a pewter spoon. A solitary canoe — fisherman, alone — moves so slowly it might be drawn. I stop to watch and lose four minutes I had not planned to lose.

06:31
A man on a bicycle, no hands, carrying a long pipe across his shoulders. He balances by leaning back. I want to ask him where he is going. I do not.
06:48
Light, arriving.

The sun comes up the way an old printer warms — first the orange, then everything else. The towers behind me catch it ten minutes before the water does. There is, somewhere, a phrase by Calvino about the city as a system of mirrors, but I can't find it without my phone, and I won't.

(I looked it up later. He was talking about Valdrada.)

07:10

A boy selling sachet water spots me writing. He asks if I am a student. I say yes — and it is technically still true, in some part of my life. He nods like this confirms something he had suspected. Hands me a sachet. I drink it. It costs 30 naira. We do not speak again.

07:42
End of walk.

I cross back onto the island. The traffic begins the way traffic does — not as a sound but as a pressure. My notebook is wet at the corner from the sachet. The first commuters look at me as if I am the strange one, which I suppose I am.

I do not know what walks are for. I keep doing them anyway, on the suspicion that something will eventually accumulate.
